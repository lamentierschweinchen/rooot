1 · CO-WEAVING — THREE WAYS FANS GET A HAND ON THE LOOM

SAME SEGMENT (50'–80', THE EQUALIZER) · MARKET & EVENTS REAL · FAN MARKS SPECIMEN

All three keep the honesty line: the probability field is never fan-editable. Fans mark the border (A), the commemorative bands the game opens (B), or the border's pattern (C). They compose — my recommendation is A + B together, C parked.

A · THE THREAD

PERSONAL · RARE

Each rooted fan lays one thread per match, at a minute they choose. It enters their own end's selvage and runs to full time. Gold if they hold a proved call. The story: "I laid mine at 89', during the VAR check."

ONE THREAD = ONE FAN'S CHOSEN MOMENT · START = THE MINUTE · GOLD = PROVED CALL · DENSITY BINS PRINTED AT SCALE
MY REC — LEAD

B · THE OPEN ROW

COMMUNAL · EARNED

At moments the game opens (goal, FT), a commemorative band unlocks for 60 seconds: every tap knits one visible stitch into it, from your end's side. If the crowd shows up, the band completes; if not, it stays honestly half-knit. The crowd finishes the goal's row — together, or not.

ONE STITCH = ONE TAP IN THE WINDOW · COLOR = THE TAPPER'S END · FILL = REAL PARTICIPATION · BAND EXISTS ONLY WHERE THE GAME OPENED IT
MY REC — PAIR WITH A

C · THE PATTERN VOTE

COLLECTIVE · PERIODIC

Every 15-minute block, each end votes its border's next jacquard pattern (fixed set of four, drawn from the tile references). The border becomes the crowd's collective mood record — but it's a poll wearing knitwear, and it invites menu UI onto the loom.

PATTERN = PLURALITY OF REAL VOTES (COUNTS PRINTABLE) · ONE SEGMENT = 15' OF COLLECTIVE CHOICE

2 · THE STANDS — REACT, ROAR, AND A SENTIMENT RECORD THAT'S AN OBJECT

GLYPHS ARE DRAWN, NEVER EMOJI · REACTION DATA SPECIMEN · FAITH WINDOWS REAL (FROM SCORE)
TOP — THE PANEL AT 58' (REACTION WINDOW OPEN) · BOTTOM — THE MOOD QUILT AFTER 60'
■ FAITH WINDOWS & EVENTS — REAL · ■ REACTIONS / ROAR — SPECIMEN, BINS PRINTED

HOW IT WORKS

The drum stays the heartbeat (every hit one counted tap). At drama the game opens a 30-second reaction window: four drawn glyphs — THE SPARK, THE ARROW, THE EYE, THE FRAY — ambiguous on purpose; you pick what you feel. Then the reveal: each end's counts, face to face. No scoring, no right answer, counts never percentages.

THE MOOD QUILT is the record: one tile per five minutes, in the tile-grid language of the references. Every property is a datum — the pattern is the window's dominant glyph, the two-ink split is the ends' share of reactions, pattern density is volume (binned, printed), a gold frame is a real faith window. The quilt grows all match beside the scarf: the match knits a scarf; the crowd quilts its mood. At the whistle it's a second keepsake — the stands' banner.

SPARKan eruption of feeling — the starburst tile
ARROWsurging belief — the chevron tile
EYEcan't look away — the ring tile
FRAYnerves — the scatter-dot tile
QUILTtile = 5' · split = ends' counts · density = volume · gold frame = faith
The glyph set is curatable per match (like the pulse spec wanted) — four is the ceiling, drawn pop-geometry only. Verdicts: bless the vocabulary names/forms, and whether the quilt lives beside the scarf on one scroll or behind a panel tab.

3 · THE GRAPHS — THE REST OF THE DATA, BOLD AND LABELED

NOT THE SCARF, NOT ABSTRACT — GRAPHS IN THE SAMPLE LANGUAGE · ALL MARKET/EVENT DATA REAL

Four modules, one per sample. Each has a job, a title that says it, one plain line under the title, and labeled axes — the mirror's legibility mission in the louder register you pinned.

THE RIBBON — WIN PROBABILITY, WHOLE MATCH

The market's live belief that Argentina win, stepped tick by tick — the 1990 chevron as a data object. Where the ribbon breaks, a goal. Where it ends, the market settled and died.

EVERY GOAL CASTS A SHADOW

One block per goal; the black shadow's length is how far the market moved on it (two real ticks). The extra-time goals cast no shadow — the market was already dead. That fact IS the graph.

HOW OFTEN THE MARKET SPOKE

One rod per minute, length = real price ticks that minute. The market chatters before the storm: 22 ticks at 50', 30 at 74', a silent minute at 75'. Dot tips mark goals (team ink) and your call (gold).

THE ROAR, FIVE MINUTES AT A TIME

Two quarter-circles per window, facing off — radius is that end's taps (binned, printed). Gold frames are real faith windows: cheering while behind counts double. Crowd specimen until tonight's counts.
