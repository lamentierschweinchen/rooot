THE DIAGNOSIS, THE GATE, THE BET

Today the stage carries about fifteen live numbers on tens of thousands of marks: the territory dots are pattern fill, the crowd figures are fixed art, the rails hold shapes with no wire behind them. The substrate underneath is far richer: a market that ticks ~911 times a match and dies on a level 90th minute, a wire that clocks every shot, card, VAR breath and danger spell, a stands service where every count increment is one real human, and a call receipt notarized against the curve's exact second.

Each direction below was run through the double gate — is every mark a datum, and does the object itself produce awe. Where my first schematic comps passed the data test but not the beauty test, the mock was rebuilt as material: the Loom is now actually knit, the Terraces are an actual album page, the Press is an actual bureaucratic ticket. Every market and event mark is real, from last night's five-goal epic. Crowd marks are specimen, with the bin printed on the surface.

THE GATE, APPLIED

DATA — PASS

The strongest data object of the three: the entire real curve is always on screen (911 ticks woven into 116 rows), swings are legible as color boundaries, the market's death is a visible material fact, and the keepsake needs no translation — the render loop and the relic are one object.

AWE — PASS, ON ONE CONDITION

A scarf knitting itself during a live match is the goosebump: the 58' cliff becomes a stripe you can point at forever, and after the 90' settlement the crowd keeps singing onto undyed wool — grief you can see. The condition: it must read as CLOTH. My first comp rendered bars and failed; this mock knits every stitch, letters the swings as jacquard bands, and sews the goals on as patches.

THE MOVE

Rotate the pitch's meaning: the surface is the match's own timeline, knit one row per minute. Each row's three widths are that minute's real probability triple — home ink from the left, away ink from the right, the draw as undyed wool between. The 50 % warp thread never moves. The live row is the shuttle; at the whistle the cloth lifts off the loom and is the scarf.

THE GATE, APPLIED

DATA — PASS

The purest granularity story: one cell = one human, position = join order, flash = a real tap, area = quantity (the Large Labor Model law). Nothing on the pages can be deleted without losing a person.

AWE — PASS, ON ONE CONDITION

The awe is intimate rather than sublime: a stranger's sticker slapping in seconds after they rooted, and your own cell yours forever. The condition: the page must feel like an ALBUM — numbered waiting sockets, sticker borders, patterned stickers — not a settings grid. My first comp failed that; this mock prints the sockets' waiting numbers and gives every sticker its cut edge. The sparse cold-start page is honest and I'd wear it, but it needs your eye.

THE MOVE

Flip the hierarchy: the crowd is the surface. Each end is an album page where one cell is one real rooted human, placed in join order — root, and your sticker slaps into your end while everyone watches. The match compresses to a schematic instrument band between the pages: split-bar triple, fixed 50 % seam, the tick-strip, events stamped at their minutes. The pitch goes fully schematic; the people don't.

THE GATE, APPLIED

DATA — PASS

The tape's line density is the market's own heartbeat — 8 lines a minute at 20', 22 at 50', 30 at 74', silence where it paused — a datum no chart normally shows. Events don't decorate the record, they puncture it; a punch is exactly what notarized means.

AWE — PASS, ON ONE CONDITION

The awe is the machine: watching a press chatter faster as the draw looms, and a hole appear through the display at a goal. The condition is España-grade restraint — the base must be bureaucratic-beautiful (multilingual whisper labels, big shouting numerals, pictograms), and punches rationed to goals and calls. If everything punches, nothing does.

THE MOVE

The display is a machine: a matchday press running one long ticket. The bureaucratic base — fixture strip, pictogram row, minute rules, serials — is designed once and never moves. Everything alive is variable data: the market prints as a tape whose line density is the real tick cadence, and events do not draw — they punch. Punched is forever; it is the on-chain gesture made physical.

MY CALL. Lead with THE LOOM as the stage's future: it is the only direction where the product's soul — watch becomes own — is the literal render loop, and it turns the market's full real curve (the thing no one else has) into the picture itself. Steal from THE TERRACES regardless: one-cell-one-fan is too honest to lose — it should replace the pictogram crowds in any direction. Keep THE PRESS as the relic-and-receipt language (the stub, the punch, the FT tear) rather than the live stage. One product, three fabrics: cloth to watch, stickers to belong, a ticket to keep.

For the owner: verdicts I need are (1) which direction leads, (2) whether ET rows may ever carry dye if №4 finds an honest ET market, (3) the sparse-crowd state in II — wear it or gate it, (4) the FT tear in III — ritual or gimmick. Graphic material I'd ask for: a plain striped-scarf macro (weave calibration), one album interior spread, a thermal-receipt macro (print chatter), and tonight's real 17:00 UTC crowd counts to set honest bins.
