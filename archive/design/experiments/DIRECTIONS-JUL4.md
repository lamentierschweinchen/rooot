## The double gate (owner, mid-session): data truth AND awe

Each direction was re-judged on both axes before the mockups were built. Verdicts:

- **LOOM — data PASS / awe PASS on one condition.** Strongest data object (the whole
  real curve is always the picture; the market's death is a visible material fact).
  The awe is sublime — the cloth remembers — but ONLY if it reads as cloth. The first
  schematic comp rendered bars and failed; the v2 mock knits every stitch, weaves the
  swings as jacquard letter bands, and sews goals on as patches.
- **TERRACES — data PASS / awe PASS on one condition.** Purest granularity (delete any
  mark, lose a person). The awe is intimate — you are visible, forever. Condition: it
  must feel like an ALBUM, not a settings grid; the v2 mock prints numbered waiting
  sockets, sticker cut edges, and pattern variety keyed to join index (no randomness).
- **PRESS — data PASS / awe PASS on one condition.** The tape's line density is the
  market's own heartbeat (a datum charts don't show); a punch is what notarized means.
  The awe is mechanical — the press chatters faster as the draw looms. Condition:
  España-grade restraint; punches rationed to goals + calls or nothing punches.

## I · THE LOOM — the match weaves itself

**Bet:** the market curve is the hero. The pitch becomes the match's own timeline: one
weft row per minute, the row's three widths = that minute's real triple (home ink |
draw paper | away ink). The full curve-so-far is always on screen, as cloth. LENGTH =
TIME · WIDTH = PROBABILITY · the 50% warp thread is the one constant.

- Swings print as hard color boundaries in the cloth (28' = 81→95 · 58' = 94→62).
- Goals/cards/shots/danger = stitched glyphs pinned at their minute-row, acting side.
- VAR / suspension = the row frays (held breath). 89' in the real bundle.
- Level at 90' → the 1X2 settles draw and DIES: ET rows print as BARE WARP — the match
  stitching events onto cloth the market no longer colors. The loudest quiet frame.
- Your call = a gold stitch at your minute, stamped with the curve's second.
- Crowd = the two scarf ends: one fringe strand = N rooted fans (bin printed on the
  plate), end-band sparks = decayed real taps, faith = gold thread rows while trailing.
- FT: the cloth lifts off the loom — the live surface IS the scarf relic (MatchArc is
  already this shape). Watch → own collapses to one gesture.

**Risk:** ~5px per minute-row on a phone; glyph legibility must be proven on device.
**Reference DNA:** manu scarf · RL flag patchwork · olympics chevron rows · scarves rack.

## II · THE TERRACES — every fan is a sticker

**Bet:** the crowd is the hero and the surface. Each end is an album page: ONE CELL =
ONE ROOTED HUMAN, placed in join order, empty sockets printed (got/need). Rooting slaps
your sticker in live. Cheers flash cells at the real decayed tap rate — the roar is made
of visible thumbs. Faith = the trailing page's gold border + CHEERS COUNT ×2 plate.
Gold pip on a cell = that fan holds a notarized call. Bins re-print honestly as counts
grow (1 CELL = 25 → 50 → …, stated on the page plate like an edition line).

The match compresses to a schematic instrument band between the pages: split-bar triple
with the fixed 50% seam, goal-arch pictograms, tick-strip (one stroke = one real market
minute), event punches at their minutes. The pitch goes fully schematic; the people don't.

FT verdict = the two filled pages face to face; your cell ringed (END ARG · CELL Nº 8).
The attendee Merkle root already promised in PRODUCT.md is literally this page's
printing plate.

**Risk:** sparse pages before scale (ten fans = ten cells). I'd wear it — needs the
owner's eye on the honest cold-start state.
**Reference DNA:** panini album/pack · pennant flag-mosaic · badge sash · Topps grounds ·
Large Labor Model's law (area = quantity).

## III · THE MATCHDAY PRESS — the match prints its own ticket

**Bet:** events + receipts are the hero; the display is a machine. A bureaucratic
designed base (España-82 grid, Mexico-70 pictograms, serials) never moves; everything
alive overprints as variable data — the US-94 split made structural.

- The tape: the market curve prints downward, LINE DENSITY = REAL TICK CADENCE
  (8/min at 20', 22 at 50', 30 at 74' — the market audibly speeds up as the draw looms;
  0-tick minutes print as real gaps).
- Events don't draw, they PUNCH: goal = a hole through the ticket (irreversible = the
  on-chain gesture made physical); card = square notch; sub = double notch; shot/corner
  = edge pips; possible-goal that dies = dotted pre-punch printed VOID (4' real).
- Your call = the gold-rimmed punch + "41' · THE WORLD SAID 1%" impact-printed.
- Suspension/settlement: the print head halts visibly; "90' LEVEL — 1X2 SETTLES · DRAW"
  plate; ET punches land on unprinted tape.
- Crowd = tally rails at the page edges (1 pip = N rooted, bin printed) + tap sparks.
- FT = the TEAR: the ticket separates at the perforation (the one legal tooth) — the
  stub the relic bench already prints, earned live.

**Risk:** the most literal — needs España-grade restraint or it drifts novelty. Punches
rationed to goals + calls only.
**Reference DNA:** mexico 70 stub (data as punch-holes) · españa 82 grid + seiko voice ·
us 94 impact print · 1966 rotated rail.

## My call

Lead with **THE LOOM** as the stage's future — it makes watch-becomes-own the literal
render loop and turns the full real curve (the thing nobody else has) into the picture.
Steal THE TERRACES' one-cell-one-fan for the ends in any direction — it should retire
the pictogram crowds outright. Keep THE PRESS as the receipt/relic language (stub,
punch, FT tear) rather than the live stage. Cloth to watch, stickers to belong, a
ticket to keep.
