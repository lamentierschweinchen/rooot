# COLD EYES — PASS 2
*A football fan opens loom-proto.html knowing nothing. Written straight after, plain voice.*

*(Honesty note: my browser tab refused to animate on its own — everything below was watched by dragging the timeline slider through the match. Where I say a button "did nothing," that might be my frozen tab, not the product. Flagged where it matters.)*

---

## FIRST 10 SECONDS

"THE LIVING LOOM." A scoreboard: ARG 0–0 CPV, red LIVE dot, 0', 1ST HALF, POSS ARG 82%. Below it, a huge empty dark frame with a gold border that says **THE MATCH NOT YET WOVEN**.

I thought: this is a live World Cup match — Argentina vs Cape Verde — that hasn't kicked off yet, and this site is going to *weave the match into a piece of cloth* as it happens. That cold open is genuinely great. The empty loom plus "not yet woven" made me sit down and wait for kick-off like it was a telly.

Two wobbles in those same ten seconds: the top-right corner quietly says PROTOTYPE · REPLAY while the scoreboard flashes LIVE — took me a while to be sure which one was lying. And POSS ARG 82% at minute zero is a strange thing to be told.

The moment it fully clicked was full time: **"THE SCARF IS YOURS · ARG 3–2 CPV."** The word *scarf* did more explaining than anything else on the page. It's not a chart. It's a matchday scarf being knitted by the match itself. I'd wear the thing.

---

## WHAT I GOT INSTANTLY

- **It's a football match.** Scoreboard, score, minute, half, possession. Broadcast furniture, zero learning needed.
- **The cloth grows with the match.** Minute marks down the left (5', 10', 15'...), a little gold triangle at the weaving edge — the "now". Empty at 0', full at 124'. Watched it for one jump (0'→15') and never doubted it again.
- **Ball patches = goals.** An embroidered football badge appears, a gold thread runs right across the full width of the cloth at that minute, the scoreboard ticks over, and there's a faint "ARG 28'" stitched above it. Goals as commemorative patches sewn onto a scarf is exactly how goals should work. Each team's badge is in its own colours (sky/white ball for ARG, dark starry ball for CPV) so I always knew whose goal it was without reading.
- **Blue vs dark ground = who the match belongs to.** Not the exact metric — but the *feeling*. ARG scores at 28' and the dark column on the right gets strangled thin. CPV equalise at 58' and the dark floods diagonally across the cloth like spilled stout. That wedge IS how a comeback feels in your stomach. Later the tag's "CLIFF" chart (93 → 41) confirmed I'd read it right.
- **Goal frame with a red X = a chance gone.** Universal. Goal frame with a little ball in it = on target (kept out) — got that one after the gloves page (below).
- **Corner flags on the edges = corners**, on your own team's side of the cloth. Tiny flag on a pole. Done.
- **The TV icon at ~89' with the dashed gold line = VAR.** A dashed line — a line that doesn't fully count — plus a telly. I laughed. Between that, the chip named "90' DEATH", and ARG scoring at 92', I reconstructed the whole near-death sequence without a single word of explanation.
- **THE COUNT (the hanging tag), once opened, explains itself brilliantly.** Keeper gloves with the saved balls floating above them (1 vs 7 — their keeper had a worldie). Corner flags with one swooshed arc per corner (9 vs 5). A brick wall for blocked shots. Off-target shots drawn as balls sitting *in the crowd* in the stands. "66% OF THE MINUTES." A barcode of the match with one green spike: "Minute 105 boiled. 38 actions." I understood every one of these on sight. Best stats page I've ever scrolled, no contest.
- **STAND WITH ARG / STAND WITH CPV = pick your end.** And it pays off: after I stood with ARG and tapped a feeling, it showed "YOUR END: ARG — FIRE 3,891 / THEIR END: CPV — PRAYER 1,204". Our end blazing, their end praying. Emotional, funny, instantly legible. Best moment of the bottom panel.
- **CHEER.** Mashed it eight times, our end went from 1,900/s to 2,196/s and the gold bar grew. I was counted. Satisfying — even silent.
- **DAY mode.** Pressed it expecting a light theme, got the *daylight colourway of the scarf* — cream where the dark was, the white cord turned black, suddenly it's merch you could buy outside the ground. This one button is secretly the sales pitch: oh, this ends as an object.
- **The replay deck reads as a replay deck.** Play/pause swap correctly, speeds, a scrubber, and chapter chips with pub names — KICK-OFF, 58' CLIFF, 90' DEATH. Naming chapters after how they felt, not just minutes, is very right.

## WHAT I GUESSED WRONG OR COULDN'T READ
*Ranked by how badly it blocked me.*

1. **The two cords. The single biggest blocker.** Two ropes snake down the entire scarf — clearly the spine of the whole picture — and I never confidently learned which was which. First guess: white = possession. Then the header said POSS ARG 8% while both cords hugged the ARG side, and my theory died. At 62' (POSS ARG 100%, white cord far left) it un-died. Eventually I found **THE KEY** — one line of tiny text at the very bottom of the page, under the debug deck — saying "possession (fine cord) · pressure (bold cord)". Even armed with that: which one is *fine*? They're both rope-thick; I had to squint at two screenshots side by side to decide the gold one is thinner. And the key never says which *side* of the cloth means which team. **What would have helped, at the moment of confusion:** the cords introducing themselves once — a tiny stitched label at the top of each ("THE BALL", "THE PRESS") — or a touch on the cord answering with a name. Not a legend in the basement.
2. **The gray blocks down the right rail.** Outside the gold border, all match long, different sizes. I had no theory at all. The key says "tempo (rail)" and I *still* only half-believe I've matched the words to the blocks (busier spell = bigger block?). They're the same gray as scrollbars and debris, which made me half-dismiss them as UI junk rather than match data. One word at the top of the rail would fix it.
3. **The striped white boards on the cloth.** Little ladder/venetian-blind rectangles at 7', 48', 68', 86', 97', 120'. My guess: substitution boards (they look like the ref's LED board, and 48'/68'/86' are classic sub minutes) — but the one at 7' rattled me, the one at 120' sits right on top of a missed-chance X so they read as one confusing blob, and nothing anywhere confirms or denies. THE COUNT has no subs section either. I finished the session still not *knowing*.
4. **The chevron stitches (^ v ^ v groups).** Blue groups on the left in hot ARG spells, red ones on the right in hot CPV spells. Attacks? Crowd surges? Shots charged down? They clearly carry mood but I couldn't cash them into a fact, and the key doesn't mention them at all. The only mark I'd call fully opaque.
5. **The pink strip on the right edge.** For half the session I read it as "danger building" — it's red, it swells in bad moments. Only after CHEER showed me CPV's end is *louder* (2,240/s vs our 1,900/s) did I re-read it as *their crowd's noise*, woven as the edge of the cloth. Lovely idea — but red-means-threat is a strong prior, and my own end's strip on the left is so thin/dark I'm still not sure it exists. If my cheering visibly fattened MY edge for a beat, I'd have learned the whole system in one tap.
6. **LIVE vs REPLAY.** The scoreboard pulses ● LIVE in red; the corner says REPLAY; the provenance stamp says the fixture was 03 JUL 2026 (three days ago). For a good minute I believed I'd walked into a live match. One honest word where LIVE is — "REPLAY" or "AS LIVE" — ends the doubt.
7. **Half-time is invisible.** I went looking for the seam between the halves and there is none — 45' just flows into 46'. A scarf of a match wants a visible fold or a fat plain band at the interval (the VAR dashed line proves special lines are in the vocabulary). Extra time also starts unannounced on the cloth itself.
8. **"THE CROWD" means two different things.** The panel called THE CROWD contains "THE CROWD SAYS 2–1" *and* "THE MARKET, NOW 84 · 12 · 4 IN 100 · H D A". Inside the tag, a row called THE CROWD showed only dashes. H D A is bookie-speak — I got there, but through the betting coupon in my head, not through fan language. Money and hearts are genuinely different beliefs (the tag even says so: "Money · hearts · reality") — the panel mushes them into one box.
9. **The prediction rules feel decorative.** "PREDICTIONS OPEN UNTIL KICK-OFF" — yet at the 112th minute, having *watched* the goals go in, I set 3–2, pressed NAME IT, and it politely accepted: "NAMED 3–2 · LOCKS AT KICK-OFF." That's me robbing the bookies. Either stop me, or wink at me ("replay — names don't count").
10. **My actions mostly don't move the numbers.** STAND WITH ARG lit up gold but ARG stayed exactly 8,204 — I wanted to *be* 8,205. The crowd's 2–1 ignored my named 3–2. CHEER was the only control that visibly counted me, and it's the one that felt best. Coincidence? No.
11. **"THE FINE PRINT — Prints at full time."** mid-match is a headline over nothing. Cute once you get it, but on first scroll it reads broken.
12. **"THE MATCH NOT YET WOVEN"** still sits below the cloth *after* full time. It's all woven, mate.

## WHAT I WANTED TO TOUCH BUT COULDN'T (or touched and nothing happened)

- **Every mark on the cloth.** I tapped the 103' goal badge expecting the story of the goal — scorer, the moment, anything. Nothing. Same for the X's, the cards, the TV icon, the striped boards, the flags. The scarf is one big picture and none of it answers touch (only the hanging tag does). This is the single loudest itch on the page: the marks *look like buttons* — embroidered, raised, shadowed — and they're mute.
- **The finished scarf itself.** At full time I tried to grab the cloth and pull it back down to relive the 28' goal. Can't — the window only shows the last ~25 minutes and the only way back is the time slider, which re-weaves the past instead of letting me hold the finished object and look along it. The scarf is five windows long and I've never seen it whole. Let me unroll it end to end at full time — that's the moment I'd screenshot and send to the group chat.
- **THE COUNT tag doesn't advertise itself.** It's the only tappable thing on the cloth and it does nothing to say so. I tapped it because it looks like a garment tag (great instinct-bait), but a first-timer who doesn't could leave never knowing half the product exists. A little sway, a "PULL" stitch, anything.
- **The chapter chips.** KICK-OFF, 28' ARG, 58' CLIFF... I tapped them and nothing visibly moved — the slider stayed put. (Probably my frozen tab — the slider itself always worked — but if a chip doesn't at least slam the scrubber to its minute instantly, it feels dead.)
- **The cords.** Wanted to run a finger down one and have it say its name. See blocker #1.
- **CHEER on the cloth.** My cheering changed a number in the panel, but I couldn't find where *my* noise landed on the scarf. If the answer is "your end's edge gets thicker," show me it happening the second I press.

## THE ONE CHANGE

**Let me tap the marks on the cloth, and answer with one stitched line.** Tap the 103' badge → "CPV GOAL · 103' — belief fell 93 → 41." Tap a striped board → "SUB — ARG." Tap a cord → "THE BALL — leaning ARG." One mechanic teaches every symbol, both cords, the wedge, the rail — everything I spent ninety minutes triangulating from three corners of the page — and it teaches it at the exact moment of curiosity, which is the only moment that counts. (If I'm allowed a free runner-up: THE KEY is the answer sheet to the whole scarf and it lives in the basement under the debug deck, in the smallest type on the page. Hang it next to the cloth like a care label.)

## THE BOTTOM DECK — PRODUCT OR DEBUG?

Mixed, leaning product. PLAY/PAUSE, RESTART, the scrubber, and above all the named chapter chips (58' CLIFF, 90' DEATH — proper pub names for chapters of a match) feel like a thing fans were meant to hold: a replay deck, the match as a DVD. But the row also has a workbench air: "PROTOTYPE" in the corner, terminal-looking 2×/8×/30× toggles, and THE KEY squashed underneath like a footnote. DAY/NIGHT is the odd one out — that's not a control, that's the scarf's *colourway*, arguably the most product-y feature on the page, hiding between RESTART and the speed buttons. Verdict: 70% product, 30% maker's bench, and the 30% is mostly about where things sit, not what they do.
