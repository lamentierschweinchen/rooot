# ROOOT — POP LANGUAGE (the pivot distillation)

*The world turns LOUD. Retro Bauhaus-meets-Panini: colorful, cartoon-design fun rather
than realistic, ALIVE. This file replaces the moody night look as the visual north star.
Every hex below was sampled by eye off the reference images, not invented. This is a
STUDY — the coordinator writes the design prompt FROM it. No decisions locked here except
where a reference makes the rule objective.*

**One line:** flat saturated grounds owned one-at-a-time (the Topps rule), hard black keylines
+ white borders, one diagonal max, pop-geometry glyphs (the Mexico-70 ball), pictogram rows,
flag-block patchwork, honest offset print texture — the honest geometry survives as **flat
color fields with hard working edges**, not glow.

**How the two teams' colors slot per fixture (without fighting the ground):**
The loud ground is chosen from the rotation to be **a color neither team owns** (or Terrace Grey
if both teams are loud). Team colors then enter ONLY as: (1) the two **flag-blocks / patchwork
panels** at the belief ends, (2) the **keyline-boxed team chips** in the score band, (3) the
scarf/relic ends. They never become the ground and never become UI chrome. If a team pair
would clash with a candidate ground, rotate the ground, not the team. Press Black + Newsprint +
one Medal-Gold accent are the constant that lets any two team palettes coexist.

**Ground-per-surface starting map (coordinator to confirm):** stage belief field → Grass or the
neutral, team color in the *tide/territory* not the pitch · match card → rotates per fixture
(Topps) · call stub → Azteca Sun band + Newsprint data block + Magenta header · scarf → Press
Black colorway, team blocks at ends · trophy vitrine → Newsprint shelves, Medal-Gold centerpiece.

## B · SHAPE GRAMMAR (the recurring geometry)

For each: where it appears · proportional rule · which surface it maps to.

1. **Thick black keyline + white/cream border** — *Topps cards, badges, 66 ticket.* Keyline ≈
   **1.5–2.5% of card width** (a fat hairline, always visible at thumb size); white border ≈
   **4–6%** of width outside it. → **cards, stubs, badges, every framed relic, UI cards/chrome.**
2. **Flat saturated ground, cutout figure** — *every Topps card.* The subject sits on ONE flat
   field; no gradient, no scene. → **stage belief fields, card grounds, badge grounds.**
3. **Diagonal display band / racing stripes — ONE diagonal max** — *Topps team-name bar, Futbol
   86.* A single fat diagonal (~12–18° off horizontal) carries the loud type; if stripes, they
   run parallel. Never two competing diagonals. → **card team name, goal/verdict banners, splash.**
4. **Zigzag / scalloped edges** — *US 94 perforation teeth, 66 notched corners, ManU knit zigzag,
   pennant fringe.* Two flavors: **perforation teeth** (uniform sawtooth, tooth ≈ 3–4% of edge
   length, marks "torn from the match") and **scallop/notch** (corner cut, marks a stub/ticket).
   → **stub edges, the "draw" band edge, tab dividers, transitions.**
5. **Pop-geometry ball glyph** — *Mexico-70 stub + 1970 pennant (ball inside the "0").* A soccer
   ball abstracted to a flat black pinwheel/pentagon disc, ZERO leather realism. The house glyph.
   → **the middle O's of R-OOO-T, loading, score dot, favicon.**
6. **Concentric / radiating circles (op-art)** — *Wyman Mexico-70 lineage; the merit-badge ring
   borders; España guilloche.* Rings radiating from a source. **This rhymes exactly with our
   existing roar-rings** — the pivot keeps the roar mark, just re-inks it flat + loud. → **roar
   pulse, badge borders, the "call" ripple.**
7. **Pictogram row** — *Mexico-70 (turnstile / gate / seat-grid / clock icons), badges (one icon
   each).* A rank of tiny flat single-color glyphs, evenly gridded, one concept each. Discipline:
   **one place, one icon, one name.** → **stub info row (END/ROW/SEAT), stat legends, nav icons.**
8. **Flag-block patchwork** — *Ralph Lauren knit, pennant flag-mosaic borders, Panini tricolor
   block.* Team/nation identity as rectangular color BLOCKS assembled in a grid — never a crest.
   → **the two belief ends, scarf ends, per-fixture team identity, collection borders.**
9. **Halftone photo/figure treatment** — *Zidane collage, 66 duotone red panel.* Photographic or
   figurative content rendered as flat-toned halftone in 1–2 ink colors, cut into blocks. This is
   **how the fan's "portrait" (their data) can be figurative without a face or a likeness.**
   → **the honest portrait on the card; any imagery ROOOT ever shows.**
10. **The grid vitrine** — *PSG/Bayern cabinets, the merit-badge sash.* A rigid rectangular grid
    of cells, one relic per cell, small plaque under each, one centered emblem. → **trophy case,
    album pages, the badge sash view.**
11. **Rosette / medallion** — *knitting-pattern covers, España stub clock disc.* Pleated concentric
    ring form, printed center. → **achievement badges, the roar-mark's relic form.**
12. **Rotated type rail** — *66 "ADMIT ONE" vertical.* A band of display type turned 90°. → **stub
    spine, side labels, "CALLED IT" rail.**

## C · LINE & GRID DISCIPLINE (rules that make placement objective)

*The owner's complaint: "not good at getting the lines where they belong." So: rules, not taste.*

1. **Everything lives in a hard rectilinear block grid.** The Mexico-70 stub is 4 blocks; the US
   94 stub is 3 panels; Topps is border→ground→bar→strip. **Draw the boxes first, fill second.**
   No floating elements — every element is inside a keylined cell or ON a band.
2. **Keyline weight is a system, not a guess.** Pick one unit `k` (≈2px at card scale). Outer
   keyline = `k`. Inner rules/dividers = `0.5k`. Band separators = `k`. Never mix arbitrary weights.
3. **White/cream border is mandatory and uniform** around any framed object (Topps law). Same
   width all four sides. It is the breathing room that lets loud grounds not scream into each other.
4. **One diagonal maximum per composition.** Everything else is orthogonal. A diagonal is a
   special event (team name, goal). Two diagonals = clipart chaos (see Anti-list).
5. **Type sits ON a band or INSIDE a box — never floating on the ground.** Topps names sit on the
   keyline bar; stub data sits in the cream block; the diagonal carries the display word. If text
   has no band/box, it's misplaced.
6. **Alignment is to the grid edges, always.** Labels hang to the left rule; numbers to the right
   rule (US 94: SECTION left, price right). Pictogram rows are evenly distributed, not eyeballed.
7. **Legibility-through-contrast is a hard gate:** loud ground ⇒ type is black, white, or the
   *contrasting* loud (never a near-value neighbor). Test at thumbnail; if the word blurs into the
   ground, the pairing is illegal.
8. **Busy is fine IF gridded.** The pennant is dense but readable because every flag is a same-size
   cell in a border rank. Density ≠ chaos when the grid holds.

## D · PRINT TEXTURE (press-language, not fake-aging)

- **Halftone dots:** visible on the Zidane skin-blocks and the 66 duotone panel. Use as a **tonal
  fill for any photographic/figurative area** and for belief-density if desired. Dot scale:
  coarse enough to read as dots at normal size (≈ the 66 panel, not fine magazine screen). One or
  two ink colors only.
- **Offset grain / flat spot ink:** the pennants and Panini pack show flat spot-color inks with a
  faint paper tooth. Reproduce as a subtle uniform grain on grounds — the "printed on paper" feel,
  the same register the old night look carried, now on LOUD paper.
- **Misregistration:** the Panini pack and pennants show slight CMYK offset (the color plates not
  perfectly aligned — a 0.3–0.8% shift, a thin fringe of pure color at edges). Use **sparingly and
  deliberately** as an accent (a hair of magenta/cyan offset on a keyline) — it reads "real press,"
  charming. Overuse = kitsch.
- **THE RULE:** texture = *press language*, never *aging*. **NO sepia, NO scratches, NO torn/burnt
  paper, NO coffee stains, NO faux-vintage filters.** The references that read premium (Topps,
  Mexico-70, Zidane) are CLEAN prints in saturated ink. The ones that read cheap-nostalgic are the
  physically-worn photos — we take their *design*, not their *wear*. Crisp saturated ink + honest
  dot/grain = the target. If it looks distressed, it's wrong.

## E · MOTION IMPLICATIONS (thinking only — not building)

1. **Squash & stretch on the loud objects** — the Anybody Os already stretch under the thumb; carry
   that cartoon elasticity to chips, badges, the ball glyph (it can squash on a "bounce").
2. **Snap eases, not smooth glides** — pop is punchy: fast-in overshoot-settle (a card SLAPS down
   like a sticker), no long dreamy fades. Cartoon timing = anticipation → snap → tiny settle.
3. **Goal = starburst + type explosion** — the "GOOOL" energy: a radiating-circle burst (roar-rings
   at max), the display word erupting in a loud ground, one-frame color flash. The fire-plume beat
   (halftime show) becomes a **starburst bloom**, not a rendered flame.
4. **Sticker-peel transitions** — panels/cards enter/exit as if peeled off a sheet (a corner lifts,
   the whole thing snaps in). The Panini-pack lineage: relics literally arrive "from the pack."
5. **Flag-wave ripple** — belief-end flag-blocks ripple like cloth on change; a shallow sine, not a
   flap. Carries the fabric register into motion.
6. **Halftone-density tide** — belief shifts as the **dot density migrating** across the field
   (dots crowd toward the leading team), a printed tide replacing the golden light tide.
7. **Punch / stamp** — a "CALLED IT" or verdict lands like an ink stamp (the punch-holes of the
   Mexico stub): quick scale-down + a tiny rotation + settle, with a dot-matrix "impact."
8. **Roar as radiating rings** — the incumbent roar-mark stays; it now pulses as flat concentric
   op-art rings (Wyman) rather than soft glow — a hard rhythmic ping outward on each cheer.
9. **Crowd-as-starfield → dot-field** — the phone-light starfield (Rihanna) becomes a field of flat
   ink dots that twinkle/shift with real cheer counts.
10. **One-diagonal wipe** — scene changes ride the single diagonal band across (Futbol-86 stripe as
    a transition swipe), keeping the "one diagonal max" law even in motion.

## G · ANTI-LIST (what makes retro-pop read cheap — named and BANNED)

- **Worn-out emoji-adjacent shapes** — clip-art soccer balls, generic star bursts, speech bubbles,
  🔥/⚽/🎉 as literal glyphs. Our ball is the Mexico-70 pinwheel, drawn; our star is the US-94 star,
  drawn. Never a stock/emoji shape.
- **Default comic fonts** — Comic Sans, Bangers, Luckiest Guy, any "fun" webfont default. The type
  is Anybody/Young Serif/Doto/Silkscreen. Fat grotesque = Topps voice, not a joke font.
- **Gradient abuse** — this world is FLAT SPOT INK. No soft radial glows, no drop-shadow blur, no
  gradient meshes, no "glassmorphism." Depth comes from flat keylines + white borders, not blur.
  (The old glow look is the thing we're leaving — don't smuggle it back as a gradient.)
- **Sticker-bomb chaos** — random overlapping stickers at jaunty angles filling every gap. The
  references are GRIDDED and calm-framed. One diagonal max; everything in a cell.
- **Fake distress** — sepia washes, torn edges, coffee rings, scratched/burnt paper, halftone cranked
  to "grunge," faux-Instagram vintage filters. Texture is clean press language only (§D).
- **Rainbow-everything** — using all 7 loud grounds at once. The Topps rule is ONE loud ground per
  surface. A page with five loud grounds fighting = amateur.
- **Over-illustrated badges** — the `nature patches` failure: cramming a detailed scene into a small
  patch. One icon, one name, flat (the `patches`/California register), never a mini-mural.
- **Crests / rings / club marks / player faces / FIFA marks** — still banned (AGENTS.md §4 + honesty).
  Identity is flag-BLOCKS and color-pairs, never a coat of arms; the portrait is DATA, never a face.

## Type-system cross-check (fit vs tension)

- **It fits, strongly.** Topps/Panini display bands ARE fat grotesques → **Anybody** (variable width)
  is exactly that voice, and its stretch doubles as the goal/scream motion. **Doto** is the printer's
  dot-matrix — the literal Mexico-70/US-94 impact-printed variable-data voice — perfect. **Silkscreen**
  = the ManU pixel-jacquard, 1:1 on relics.
- **One real tension: Young Serif.** The pop references are almost entirely SANS (grotesques + the
  bureaucratic gothics). Young Serif's warmth belongs to the *programme/verdict* register, not the loud
  card grounds — keep it OFF the pop surfaces (no serif on a Topps band) and reserve it for editorial
  moments, or the pivot's loudness and the serif's warmth will read as two brands.
- **Watch-item:** Doto/Silkscreen dots must stay legible against loud grounds — they're low-contrast dot
  forms; enforce §C-7 (black/white/contrasting-loud only) or numbers vanish into a hot ground.
