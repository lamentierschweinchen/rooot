READING THE RAIL

Four things the crowd does

THE ENDS — you root one side for 90 minutes (bars are counts, never a percentage). A no-affiliation watcher adopts an end instead of a third neutral bucket — the two-sided geometry stays honest.

THE CROWD — everyone names a final score at entry, stamped against the market. Its aggregate is the third belief beside market and result: "the crowd says 2–1, and it's 0.4 goals braver than the money." The doubters-within-a-fanbase cut is content nobody else has — our own data, free and honest.

THE PULSE — the six feelings you gated. It opens on most big moments (goal, VAR, red) for a ~25s window and the split reveal — and every ~5 minutes in the quiet, via a cool-off bar, so we capture the rolling mood between events too. Its aggregate is the sparks along the cloth's selvage — FEEL, the third belief.

THE ROAR — hold to cheer; the meter is loudness per end — win, lose or draw, no multipliers. It's the one crowd act that touches the cloth directly: the selvage sparks brighten.

Gate: is this the right four, in the right order? Anything that should be louder, quieter, or cut? Next I wire it to the live loom so the rail moves with the match.
