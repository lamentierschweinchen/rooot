# PAPER & CLOTH — the site language (the law)

*Owner-approved direction (Jul 5, design session). Synthesizes the loom lineage
(woven cloth), the pop/print lineage (SYSTEM.md · POP-LANGUAGE.md · archive/design-docs-consumed/design/BRIEF-PRINT-SOUL.md),
the mechanism schema (docs/MECHANISMS.md), and the sentiment lens (docs/SENTIMENT.md)
into ONE world for rooot.club. Where this doc and a rendered surface disagree, the
owner's gated sheet wins, then this doc is corrected.*

**Quality bar (owner's words):** highest quality and fidelity · true-to-nature ·
real collectible, intimate feel throughout. The word to beat is still DELIBERATE.

---

## 1 · THE LAW

Every element on every surface declares exactly one register:

- **PAPER documents.** Tickets, margins, scorecards, stubs, the album, every number,
  every label. Paper renders through `archive/src-spa-frozen/apps/web/src/lib/ink.ts` print physics — fat
  benday, ink-not-fill, warm vignetted `paperField`, the 8:4:2 frame/panel/detail
  weight scale (theme.ts WEIGHT). Paper moves only by STEPPING: registration snaps,
  stamp beats, card flips. Paper never breathes.
- **CLOTH lives.** The match, allegiance, the things you keep to wear. Cloth renders
  through the loom's physics — warp/weft interlacement, per-pick thread crowns,
  dye-lot drift, couched cords, hand-loom wobble (WOB), selvages. Cloth BREATHES:
  the weave scroll, thread settle. Cloth never steps.
- **THE SEAM RULE.** Cloth is always MOUNTED ON PAPER — tipped in like a specimen,
  held by printed corner slots. Paper frames cloth; cloth never frames paper.
  The site is an album someone keeps. That is the intimacy.
- **SENTIMENT MATERIALS (docs/SENTIMENT.md, binding):**
  BET = the woven tide (cloth body) · BELIEVE = pins pressed into cloth (hard
  objects, never thread) · FEEL = sparks at the selvage (surface light) ·
  HAPPEN = sewn/woven event marks + the printed record. Never blended, ever.
- **HONESTY (unchanged, above everything):** every dot means something; nothing
  renders that didn't happen; counts never shown as %; market ≠ crowd; no fake
  aging, no blur, no soft shadows, no decoration gradients. Material character is
  print/loom PHYSICS, not filters.
- **THE COLLECTIBLE LAW (owner, Jul 7 — binding at every altitude):** every stat,
  every page, every moment is a COLLECTIBLE — a Panini-grade object someone wants
  to show. The test for any piece: cropped out alone and sent to a friend, does it
  still carry its full story and its full craft? Requirements: a complete
  self-contained composition (survives the crop), a quiet provenance whisper
  (fixture · minute · Nº where earned), material presence (patch/print, never
  screenshot-of-a-dashboard), and one strong silhouette. Some moments literally
  crystallize into keepsakes (the scarf, the stubs, the quilt tiles) — but the
  standard applies to ALL of it: "great insights meet great design" at every turn.
  This is what the Mirror gets right; it is the bar, not a feature.

## 2 · TOKENS & MATERIALS

- **Grounds:** NEWSPRINT (theme.ts NEUTRALS cream, paperField-treated) and
  PRESS-BLACK (night — the DEFAULT this tournament; all euro-timezone kickoffs are
  night). The loom already carries both (SKIN day/night); paper surfaces get the
  same two dyes. Team inks per fixture from the teams data (coordinator has all 48).
- **THE HOST PALETTE (owner steering Jul 6 — celebrate colour in the paper room):**
  the host nations' melange as the paper world's spot inks — VERDE `#1E7A46` (México) ·
  ROJO `#C8202A` (the tri-nation red) · AZUL `#27427F` (USA) on newsprint/blanco, in
  Bauhaus builds with Lichtenstein restraint (flat spot ink, no gradients).
  **Dosage law:** host colours dress the ROOM (module bands, plate frames, page
  geometry, headers) and colour NON-TEAM data plates; **team ink alone carries
  team data** (a plate with a team split gets an ink frame, never a host frame);
  GOLD stays reserved for the exceptional (goals, the cliff, the seal). Panini
  carries the warmth (sticker plates, badge shapes); Bauhaus carries the build.
- **Type:** Anybody (labels/display, CAPS) · Doto (every number) · Young Serif
  (the intimate register: edition lines, keepsake dedications — album + keepsakes
  ONLY) · Silkscreen (micro pixel labels inside cloth). Weight law 8:4:2.
- **Print physics:** `lib/ink.ts` primitives are the only way paper renders a dot,
  rect, line, or sheet. **Cloth physics:** the loom's weave/cord/WOB primitives are
  the only way cloth renders. One implementation each; stage-era surfaces retired
  (owner ruling Jul 5: the Loom REPLACES the stage as the live surface).

## 3 · SITE ANATOMY — six surfaces

1. **THE GATE** (entry interstitial — a ticket, paper).
   Fixture, kickoff in the fan's clock (voice.ts law), two ink end-panels, two
   claims: pick an end + name the score. The stamp shows itself:
   `STAMPED 19:58 · MARKET 61·27·12`. Header: `NINETY MINUTES. TWO CLAIMS.`
   Prediction editable to KO, then `LOCKED AT KICK-OFF`.
2. **THE MATCH** (live — the cloth fills the page body, mounted under paper).
   The Loom is the only live surface. Paper furniture: thin score/clock header +
   the STANDS RAIL as a programme margin — rooted counts as two ink bars (counts,
   never %), consensus as a printed pin at the cloth edge (`THE CROWD SAYS 2–1`),
   the cheer press (label `CHEER`; the count does the talking; loudness per end,
   win/lose/draw — the faith ×2 multiplier was CUT, owner ruling Jul 6), the PULSE
   (the six owner-generated feeling-patches — muscle/fire/fear/prayer/disappointment/
   anger-swear — built SHEET 013): a react window opens on most big moments (~25s +
   split reveal, counts per end) AND every ~5 min in the quiet via a cool-off bar,
   so FEEL is a rolling signal, not only event spikes. CALL surfaced only at spikes
   (press-and-hold; the stub prints as the receipt).
2b. **THE BACK OF THE CLOTH — THE COUNT** (the stats card; owner-directed Jul 6,
   concept gated on SHEET 014). Where all the other data goes: every scarf has a
   back. A small woven tab (the care label, `THE COUNT`) lives at the cloth's
   edge; pull it and the match TURNS OVER — a hard two-frame paper flip, no 3D
   swoosh. The back is a printed page in the host palette. Rejected alternatives,
   for the record: HUD overlay (violates cloth purity), sidebar (breaks the
   one-object law). **Every stat is a picture whose geometry carries the number**,
   the number always huge and legible beside it (Doto). Pictogram law (from the
   infographic-inspo gate): counts render as REPEATED BOLD UNITS you can count,
   and peaks use the one-exception device (a field of alike marks, one in colour).
   The gated set (owner rulings Jul 6): corners = quarter-arcs growing with the
   count, flags at the origin · SAVED = the keeper's GLOVE ALONE, one ball arced
   above per save · blocked = THE WALL — a brick laid per block, count the courses ·
   off target = INTO THE STANDS — the wild shots landed among terrace crowd-dots ·
   possession = two wound spools joined by the gold thread · THE BOOKINGS = the
   referee's open notebook with REAL NAMES from the wire's own lineups roster
   (Pina 68' · Montiel 115'; scorers name themselves the same way) · THE CLIFF =
   the market's hardest fall, stepped · THE FRANTIC MINUTE = the tempo burst ·
   THE THREE BELIEFS = market/crowd/result, never blended.
   **THE SCROLL** — the back is one importance-ordered page: 1 THE HEADLINE
   (score · beliefs · cliff) → 2 THE GOAL MOUTH → 3 THE TERRITORY → 4 THE BOOK &
   THE BENCH → 5 THE FINE PRINT (quiet tabular ledger rows — deliberately less
   pictorial) → 6 THE PROVENANCE (hallmark row). Picture-density decays with
   importance; live numbers tick in place; at FT the scroll freezes into the
   keepsake's second page. Subs plate joins when the wire dedupe lands.

2c. **THE GROUND — navigation as stadium anatomy** (owner-directed Jul 6, gated
   on SHEET 014 v2). During a match there is ONE page laid out like the ground:
   the SCOREBOARD (paper header) above · THE CLOTH as the pitch (THE COUNT flips
   IN PLACE via the care-label tab — it is a face, not a destination) · THE
   STANDS below, reached by scrolling down into the crowd. NO TAB BAR. The
   masthead carries two woven tags, the only exits: **THE CASE** (the album) and
   **YOUR SEAT** (the fan: matches attended as stubs, personal scorecards, later
   YOUR ROW — friends/connections). Live match on → the site opens straight onto
   the ground; nothing on → the next gate + the case.
3. **THE PRESSING** (full time — the ceremony).
   The cloth stops, the selvage seals gold, the object is pulled through the
   press: scarf first, then the paper record stacks — scorecard
   (`YOU CALLED 2–1 · IT ENDED 2–1 · EXACT`), the end's collective card, call
   stubs, the mood-quilt tile. One share object + link. `FULL TIME · THE SCARF IS
   YOURS` stays. The FT ROSETTE (owner-generated) is the press's seal.
4. **THE ALBUM** (collection — a paper room).
   Real album grammar: pages, tipped-in scarf strips (corner slots), stubs in
   slots beneath, edition lines in Young Serif. Empty slots are promises:
   `USA–BEL · JUL 7 · 21:00 — UNWOVEN`. Got/need per tournament fixture. This is
   the retention room; it ships in v1 (owner scope ruling: Match + Album).
5. **THE LOBBY** (idle state). Next gate + last pressing + album door. When a
   match is live the site IS the match (coordinator's live-mode switch, built).
6. **THE BACK PAGE.** How it works, the honesty law, chain receipts. Quiet.

## 4 · THE EVENT LEXICON — three tiers by material truth

**Owner's bar (Jul 5): events are either beautifully WOVEN INTO the loom as it
generates, or they become vignettes like the goals. Weak stitches don't ship.**

**LEGIBILITY LAW (owner steering, Jul 5 evening — binding, supersedes the
technique-first table below for information marks):** we design for the common
soccer fan — intelligible first, visually satisfying always; artistic
sophistication is imbued in the work, never the point. Two consequences:
1. **Two layers.** INFORMATION marks use soccer's own icons — the ball, the goal
   frame, the card, the sub arrows, the corner flag, the spot, the referee's
   screen — rendered as stitchwork so they live in the cloth. No fan should ever
   need a legend. THE WEAVE beneath (slubs, dye-lot, the red-card narrowing,
   tempo) is the connoisseur layer: character, not required reading. Fouls are
   demoted to weave texture — fans don't track fouls as marks.
2. **Duration-true shapes.** A mark's vertical extent = the event's real time.
   A shot is seconds → a compact one-pick mark, never a long line (the Sheet-010
   brocade float misread as duration — retired). VAR/injury span their real
   picks. Persistent states (a booking, ten men) may leave a hair-thin
   persistent edge line — quiet, honest.
The Sheet-010 woven-technique vocabulary survives as LAYER-2 character and as
the *rendering finish* of the icons (satin-stitch cards, backstitch frames,
french-knot balls — stitched icons, still forming at the shuttle), not as the
information carrier. Gate: SHEET 011.

- **TIER 1 — WOVEN (structure).** Frequent events alter the weave itself, forming
  AT THE SHUTTLE as the row is laid; once scrolled, the mark is permanently IN the
  fabric. Real textile techniques only:
  | event | technique |
  |---|---|
  | shot on target | brocade float (team-ink supplementary weft), knotted tip |
  | shot off target | brocade float, frayed end |
  | shot blocked | brocade float, bar-stitch across |
  | woodwork | brocade float, double-ring knot |
  | corner | weft colour-change in the last dents before the selvage |
  | foul / stoppage | slub pick (the thread thickens one row) |
  | substitution | thread-piecing — one ply ends, a fresh ply knotted in |
  | VAR / check | ladder run — wefts held, warp exposed, then caught up |
  | penalty awarded | bound eyelet at the spot |
  | yellow card | selvage bound yellow for a stretch |
  | red card | the selvage LOSES A WARP THREAD — cloth one thread narrower to FT |
  All other catalog actions (docs/DATA.md, 42) map here or are consciously left
  to the tempo rail. The red-card narrowing is symbolic-but-material — flagged
  for deliberate owner judgment on Sheet 010.
- **TIER 2 — APPLIQUÉ (vignette).** Goals ONLY: the embroidered team ball + gold
  weft. Scarcity keeps the patch sacred.
- **TIER 3 — CEREMONY.** Off-cloth beats: GOOOOL eruption, the FT pressing.

**The gate:** SHEET 010 — THE LEXICON renders every mark forming live at the
shuttle, day + night, both team inks, with a vignette fallback beside every
borderline mark. The owner gates the vocabulary in one sitting; failures promote
to vignette, never ship weak.

## 6 · MOTION

Paper steps (snaps, stamps, flips, one hard beat at the pressing). Cloth breathes
(weave scroll, thread settle). Nothing eases decoratively: if it moves, the press
moved it or the loom wove it. Reduced-motion: static states. Budget ~2ms/frame.
