# ROOOT — Reference Brief (curate before any pixel ships)

*The STRATA lesson, applied from day one: models design from a statistical average of
"nice UI" unless pinned to real references. You curate images with your eye; a design
instance distills them into `REFERENCES.md`; every render is judged against those
images — never against vibes. Drop 3–6 images per category into
`design/references/<category>/`. Screenshots/webshots fine. Your taste is the gate.*

## What we need, and why

### 1. `night-pitch/` — the stage's ground
Floodlit pitches at night, from above (drone aerials, broadcast wides, empty stadiums).
**Why:** how grass actually reads under floodlight — the green-black falloff, the chalk
lines' luminance, the pooled light. The stage must feel like a real place at night, not
a dark-mode UI.

### 2. `tide-light/` — the belief's material
Bioluminescent surf at night · long-exposure tides from above · gold light moving on
dark water · slow lava fronts.
**Why:** the market's belief is a luminous tide with a defined front edge. These teach
how a glowing liquid boundary reads as *pressure* — not as a chart fill. This is the
single most important category.

### 3. `scarf-weave/` — the relic's cloth
Real club scarves, macro: weave texture, jacquard lettering, selvage edges, tassels,
worn/washed examples with history in them.
**Why:** the scarf must read as *cloth that happens to be true*, not a chart in costume.
Material realism is the whole bet.

### 4. `data-textile/` — the craft bridge
Jacquard/data-weaving art, tapestry timelines, embroidered notation, stitched marks.
**Why:** proof that data → textile can be art. The calls are *embroidered*, not plotted.

### 5. `ends-light/` — the crowd's material
Curvas/ends at night: phone-light seas, flare glow, tifo from across the stadium,
the mass of a stand as texture.
**Why:** the ends are crowds rendered as light. These images say what ten thousand
people look like as a single luminous body.

### 6. `type-print/` — the words' ground
Vintage matchday programmes · stadium scoreboards/dot-matrix · ticket stubs.
**Why:** the serif/mono voice needs period-true anchors, not "retro font" defaults.

### 7. `pins/` — the personal object
Enamel football pins/badges, macro; collectors' boards.
**Why:** the scorecard-as-pin needs the object language: hard enamel, metal lines,
small and dense.

### 8. `anti/` — what we must never resemble
Betting-app screens (DraftKings/FanDuel/Kalshi) · generic sports dashboards ·
"AI-gradient" landing pages.
**Why:** the design instance needs the negative space named. If a render drifts toward
these, it dies in review.

## Process
1. You fill folders (partial is fine — categories 2, 3, 5 matter most for Saturday).
2. Design instance writes `design/REFERENCES.md`: per image, what specifically to take
   (a falloff, an edge quality, a weave density) — and what to refuse.
3. Every stage/relic render ships to you *next to its references*. Judged against the
   pinned truth, iterated on your terse notes.

*Optional: say the word and an agent pre-gathers candidate images from free sources
(Unsplash/Wikimedia) into `design/references/_candidates/` for your veto — your eye
still the gate, just less hunting.*
