Where I understand we're going — correct me

ROOOT is the honest, beautiful keepsake of living a match on your phone, in a printed paper-and-cloth world — not a night pitch, not a dashboard. You root once, cheer constantly, call rarely. The market is a plain number; the crowd is a real roar, counted, never a percentage. When the whistle blows you keep what you lived — a woven scarf, a struck pin, a sealed poster — yours forever, provable on-chain, worthless to flip. The tide is gone; the loom stays. If any of that is off, that's the thing to fix before the docs — it's the north star everything else measures against.
