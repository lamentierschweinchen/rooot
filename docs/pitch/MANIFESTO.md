# THE MISSING FEED

*ROOOT — from fan experience to belief data*

---

**1 · Sports data measures everything except what the crowd believes.**
The match has never been better instrumented — every pass tracked, every price
archived to the tick, in-running markets on anything that moves — and the
largest signal in the building is still dark. What passes for crowd belief is
proxy: scraped social noise, pre-match polls that fall silent at kickoff, and
betting flow — hearts read from slips, after the stake lands, each book across
its own counter only. A pre-bet, declared-allegiance belief feed does not
exist.

**2 · The non-bettor doesn't exist in the data.**
Most fans never place a bet. The industry's entire measure of conviction runs
through the minority who do; the actual crowd is dark — not because its belief
has no value, but because nobody ever built it a reason to state it.
Conviction without a stake has nowhere to live.

**3 · Nobody collects the 89th minute.**
The market re-prices every second of a live match; the crowd's read of the
same seconds is collected nowhere. Pools and polls harvest pre-match picks by
the million, then go quiet for the ninety minutes that matter. Nobody fills
out a form mid-collapse. That is not a survey-design problem; it is a product
problem.

**4 · And the price itself pays for it.**
Every in-play number is two things fused: a probability and a bias premium —
the shading books apply for the public's heart, the push of retail flow.
De-vigging strips the margin, not the crowd's thumb on the scale. Neither side
of the counter can decompose the number in real time, because nobody measures
the heart directly. The most instrumented market in sport cannot see one of
its own two ingredients.

**5 · ROOOT is the collection instrument — built as a matchday companion.**
A stadium experience you can enter from home: pick an end, name the score, say
how sure; cheer with your end, counted against the other; call the next goal
when the collapse starts. Every claim is stamped server-side with the
de-vigged line at that second and scored at the whistle. The fan isn't sitting
on a panel — they're at the match. That is the entire trick: the ritual
collects what no survey can.

**6 · The fan keeps the night.**
At full time the night settles: calls scored against the number they defied,
and the match itself woven into a scarf — the odds' rise and collapse as bands
of cloth, the fan's claim in the weave, a matchday object rather than an app
screen — minted to a passkey made with a thumbprint. No wallet, no token, no
wager. The fan gets the night; the dataset gets the crowd. Retention isn't a
streak mechanic; it's a shelf filling with nights.

**7 · What settles at the whistle is the missing feed.**
Per match: every claim, its market stamp, its resolution — crowd splits by end
with n printed beside them, sealed on Solana at full time so nobody, including
us, can restate them after. Provably pre-outcome, because a belief series is
only worth buying if it can't have been backfilled. Three matches operated
with small, honestly counted crowds; the World Cup final sealed inside. An
instrument, not yet an index — and the index is the decomposition both sides
of the counter have been missing: how much of the number is heart, per
fanbase, per season — optimism, foresight, faith under fire, printed beside
the closing line.
