The idea

Every match, real money around the planet holds a live opinion on who wins — it trembles at every chance and lurches at every goal. It's the most dramatic signal in football, and fans never get to feel it. ROOOT turns it into weather: a floodlit night pitch on your phone where your side's light pushes against theirs, and the fog between them is the doubt. When your team's down to a 12% chance, you see the darkness. When the comeback lands, fire.

You're not watching alone. Behind each goal are the ends — everyone on Earth rooting your side, burning as one crowd of light. The louder you all cheer, the brighter you burn. The other lot are right there, pulling the other way.

Three things you do

once

Root

Pick your side — your country, your blood, or tonight's adopted underdog. You join that end. A live counter shows the two crowds massing: 12,431 v 8,207.

whenever you feel it

Cheer

One tap. Your end flares a little brighter — you can see your own tap land in the roar. Cheering while you're losing counts double. Faith is worth more than glory-hunting.

when you dare

Call it

Down a goal in the 71st minute and you feel the comeback? Press and hold — R-O-O-O-O… — release, and it's on the record with a timestamp: "Minute 71. The world said 12%. You called it." If you're right, that receipt is yours forever, and nobody in the group chat can ever dispute it again.

Us against them — even off the pitch

Your team can lose the match and your crowd can still win the stands: which end was louder, which kept the faith while behind, which called it better. There's a verdict at the whistle — "They won the match. We won the stands." — and a running table of the world's best fans across the tournament.

And you can pull your own people in: one link makes your row — up to eleven of you, your names on the same living match, talking among yourselves while the whole world roars around you.

Keep what you lived

The scarf — after the whistle, the whole ninety minutes exists as a woven scarf: the swings of belief as the stripe pattern, goals as metallic threads, your calls stitched in gold at the minute you made them, your flags in the ends. Everyone who was there gets their edition — like a crowd photo you can prove you're in.

The rosette — your personal one: your cheers, your faith through the dark spells, your receipts — pressed into an old-school club rosette.

The trophy case — every match you attended, on a shelf. Your World Cup.

All of it is free and permanently yours — saved in a way that can't be deleted, faked, or taken back, and that outlives any app. (There's serious tech underneath; you never have to touch or understand it.)

⚠️ What it's not: no betting, no money at stake, no coins to buy, nothing to trade. The only currency is being right, being loud, and being there.
