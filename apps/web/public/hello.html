01The idea

Every match, real money around the planet holds a live opinion on who wins. It's the most dramatic signal in football — and fans never get to feel it.

ROOOT draws it as a living print: your team's color advances up a paper pitch as dots — solid where the world believes, fraying into scattered specks where the belief runs out. The other lot's dots push back. The empty paper between them? That's the draw, literally. When your side is down to 12%, you see your dots crushed into a corner — and when the comeback lands, a starburst rips through the page: GOOOL.

Root

Down a goal in the 71st and you feel the comeback? Press and hold — R-O-O-O-O… — release, and it's on the record forever: "Minute 71. The world said 12%. You called it." Nobody in the group chat can ever dispute it again.

03The games off the pitch

Your team can lose the match and your crowd can still win the stands. Fanbases duel on what fans actually control:

The Stands Championship

LOUDNESS · FAITH (cheers while behind count double) · PRESENCE · FORESIGHT (calls that proved right). Verdict at the whistle: "They won the match. We won the stands." A running table crowns the world's best fans across the tournament.

The Chorus

After a big moment, a small set of stickers pops up — and you pick the one you think most of your fellow fans will pick. The crowd most in sync with itself scores. The set changes every time, so you can't game it — you just have to know your people. Terraces sing in unison; now it counts.

Your Row

One link makes your row — eleven seats for your people, your names on the same living match, talking among yourselves while the world roars around you.
