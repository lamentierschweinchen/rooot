01

Predictions, before and during

Call the final score before kickoff, then keep calling while it plays — who scores next, or nobody. Every in-play call is stamped with the market's live price at that exact second, and settled by the next real event.

What it produces: an aggregate scoreline forecast per fanbase, not just win-draw-lose — and a moving version of it as the game runs. A bookmaker prices correct-score too; the difference here is who is answering: self-declared supporters of one side, for free, with nothing staked, timestamped before the outcome.

Per in-play call: the call, the market's three prices at that instant, the minute, and how it resolved.

02

The market, in plain words

Live de-vigged win probabilities sit beside the crowd's number — visibly separate, never blended, never dressed up as each other. You can read the money's mind without ever placing a bet.

What it produces: the two readings side by side while the result is still unknown — where a fanbase sat above or below the price at kickoff, and how far apart they stayed as the game moved. The closing price is just the scoreline; the interesting part is everything before it.

WHAT IS STORED
every tick
The de-vigged home/draw/away triple on each update — three to four thousand of them across a match. Kept as the opening and closing read, a value per phase, every swing with the minute it happened, the lead changes, the volatility, and how far from an undecided line the price sat.

03

The roar, and who showed up

Cheers are rate-honest — a tap is a tap, and loudness decays like real noise. Presence is counted, not estimated: who rooted for which end, when they arrived, how long they stayed.

What it produces: attendance and attention per fanbase, a per-minute loudness curve, and arrival waves — the shape of a crowd assembling around a game.

WHAT IS STORED
who · when · how loud
Rooted count per end. Cheers that the rate limiter actually granted, never the taps attempted. Minutes of open presence per fan. First-seen time bucketed into five-minute arrival waves. A loudness sample for each end roughly every thirty seconds, all match.

THE KEEPSAKE
HOW THE NUMBERS ARE MADE

Computed live,
from the wire.

Every quantity in a match record has one definition, applied the same way every night. These are the ones worth naming.

VOLATILITY

The sum, across every price update in the match, of how far all three legs moved from the update before. A market that churns all night scores high; one that settles early scores low.

A SWING

Any moment a leg has moved six points or more away from the last price the match kept as its reference. Compared across a rolling 75-second window, so it measures real movement rather than feed chatter.

CONVICTION

How far the price sits from a completely undecided 33/33/33 line, measured on its widest leg — kept as both the average across the match and the peak.

LEAD CHANGES

How many times the favoured outcome changed hands over the match, counted on the price, not on the scoreline.

OPTIMISM GAP

Per end: the share of that end's own calls that have their side winning, minus the market's probability of that side winning at the moment predictions locked. Positive means the stand believed harder than the money did.

DOUBTER INDEX

Per end: how many people who chose that side did not call their own side to win — and, at full time, whether the doubters were right.

FORESIGHT WEIGHT

One minus the market's probability, at lock, of the result that actually happened. It scores a correct call by how unlikely the money thought it was.

POINTS

25 for a locked call, one per granted cheer to a cap of 300, two per reaction, one per minute present to a cap of 130, and at full time 200 for the exact score or 75 for the right result — multiplied by the conviction dial, up to double.

A cheer scores when the rate limiter grants it. A reaction counts once per moment, however many times it is re-picked. Minutes are open presence. The crowd's numbers and the market's stay in their own units, side by side.
