ROOOT

The World Cup as a living mechanical print — benday-ink grounds owned one at a time (loud, but Lichtenstein-subdued, not Barbie), hard black keylines, one diagonal max, honest geometry as color fields that fray into scattered dots at their edges, and every surface built to be collected. Loud, retro, cartoon-alive. Never moody, never glowing.

04

Every surface is a memento

Lean into the Panini world — not in obvious ways. You should want to collect everything you see as a poster, a card, or some other memento. So every composed surface wears print anatomy: a keyline + border, a caption / footer strip (fixture · date · frame name, in Doto), and a small serial / edition line (Nº / edition) in the printer voice. A paused stage composes as an ownable print — grounded, framed, captioned, no dangling UI. Placements for edition marks are reserved so album / got-need mechanics slot in later without a redesign.

06

The laws

Non-negotiable
  • Vertical = probability. Halfway = a thin constant Press-Black seam at exactly 50%.
  • THE DRAW is a width (= pDraw) where the two fields' dots fray to near-zero; the constant seam rides inside it. Teeth are dead — the only legal tooth is the stub perforation.
  • Territory extent = exactly win probability; solid core → dot-fray (scattered specks) at the working edge.
  • Market number ≠ crowd counts. Never blended.
  • One loud ground per surface; team colors only in the four slots. Fizz Pink is accent-only.
  • Boxes-first grid; keyline ≈2%, cream border ≈5%; one diagonal max.
  • Type on bands/boxes, never floating. Doto for numbers, Anybody for screams.
  • Press texture only — halftone, grain, a hair of misregistration.
  • Every rail cell carries a job. Goals erupt only at the real goal mouth.
  • Every surface is a memento — framed, captioned, edition-marked; a paused stage composes as an ownable print.
Banned — the diseases
  • True-hexagon soccer balls — only the 5-segment pinwheel pop-ball.
  • Speed-streaks, motion-blur — motion is stepped/snap/squash.
  • Glow, gradient mesh, drop-shadow blur, glassmorphism.
  • Sepia, scratches, torn/burnt paper, faux-vintage filters.
  • Sticker-bomb ornament — no decoration without a job.
  • Zigzag teeth as a belief edge or draw band — the draw is dot-fray; the stub perforation is the sole legal tooth.
  • Blended fray inks — two colours' stray dots stay discrete, never a muddy overlap.
  • Fizz Pink as a ground; dangling UI on a poster-ready frame.
  • Player faces, club crests, FIFA marks, trophy/silverware in match frames.
  • Two loud grounds touching; a probability in a display face; a crowd count dressed as a %.
