01 · THE ASYMMETRY

Every match produces two convictions. One is sold. The other disappears.

The money's belief is measured to the tick and licensed by a $2.13bn industry. The crowd's is gone at the whistle.

THE MEASURED HALF

Sportradar took €1.29bn in FY2025, Genius Sports $669.5m. Every tick of the market's opinion is captured, archived and licensed to sportsbooks, leagues and broadcasters.

Sportradar spent €404.3m on sport rights last year — more than its entire payroll. Genius carries $1.51bn in contracted future rights, 2.26× annual revenue.

THE MISSING HALF

What stands in for crowd belief today is scraped social noise, pre-match polls that stop at kickoff, and betting flow — which only sees people who staked, only after they staked, and only at one book.

Nobody can say what a crowd believed at the 89th minute.

WHY NOBODY HAS IT

The apps holding fan predictions can't sell them. Sofascore: "due to agreements with our data providers, we are unable to share the data sources." Sportradar's media addendum bars customers from creating "any new or derivative data points, whatsoever."

And a business that converts opinion into a wager can't sell that opinion as an independent measurement.

03 · WHO PAYS, AND FOR WHAT

Measurement sells first. The trading test comes next.

BROADCASTERS & SPONSORS

In-play graphics run on odds alone today. The gap is a story: the market gives Argentina 96%, and Argentina's end still isn't sure. For sponsors it is consented audience measurement of the fans who never bet.

Sold as a content and audience licence. No gambling adjacency.

SPORTS-DATA COMPANIES

A new product line with no rights fee, down a pipe that already reaches every trading floor. Rights are the industry's biggest cost; this data is generated by its own users, so there is no league to outbid and no exclusivity to renew.

The barrier to copying it is building a consumer product fans love first.

TRADING DESKSNEXT

Bet flow only sees people who staked. The fans who never bet are invisible to it, and they are the majority of any crowd. Whether that cohort's belief carries information on top of the de-vigged price is the question we are set up to answer.

The test: one fixture at n≥5,000, a point-in-time claim file, out-of-sample Brier score with the crowd term lagged.
