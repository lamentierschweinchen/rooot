05The mark — five directions

All five stay honest to the name: the roar, the root, the ring.

Roar ringsthe current mark, refined: rings off-center like heartwood — a roar radiating, tree rings, the ball. Already the favicon, the cheer ripple, the relic seal.
The kickoff markthe pitch's center circle + halfway line, chalk-drawn: literally 50/50 — where every match, and every belief, starts.
The screamthree Os growing — R-o-O-O-T rising like a goal call. The wordmark as the logo itself.
The rosettethe 70s club rosette as the O — ribbon pleats, button center, tails. The personal relic's own shape.
The stitchthe O as twelve knit stitches, one dropped stitch escaping — the jacquard alphabet's own mark. Reads perfectly at 16px.

My recommendation: one mark, three materials. Keep the roar rings as the primary mark — but render it in the material of wherever it lives: light on the stage (volumetric rings in fog), stitches on the scarf (the stitch variant above is the rings knitted), ink on print and the rosette button. The kickoff mark is too good to waste on a logo — it becomes the stage itself: the center circle IS drawn on our pitch, and at kickoff the camera sits on it at exactly 50/50. The ceremony, not the letterhead.

Type: pairing A. Anybody for the scream (wordmark contexts, eruptions, the call gesture animating real width), Young Serif for the programme voice (verdicts, captions, warmth), Doto for every number (scoreboard truth; Silkscreen as its knit twin on relics). Body text stays a quiet system sans — the voices above do the talking.
