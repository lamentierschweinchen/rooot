WHY THIS ORDER
Importance decays downward, picture-density decays with it: tiers 1–3 are pictogram plates (the fun, shareable layer), tier 4 is document (the book), tier 5 drops to quiet tabular rows — deliberately less pictorial so the plates above keep their weight — and tier 6 is the receipt. A fan who never scrolls still gets the whole story; the one who scrolls to the bottom finds the fine print and the proof. Live, every number ticks in place; at full time the scroll freezes as the keepsake's second page.

ONE PAGE IS THE STADIUM
During a match there is one page, laid out like the ground itself: the scoreboard above, the cloth as the pitch, the stands below it — you scroll down into the crowd, not away from the match. THE COUNT is not a destination: it is the cloth's other face, flipped in place and flipped back. No tab bar anywhere. The masthead carries two woven tags for the rooms outside the ground: THE CASE (your album — scarves on the rail, stubs beneath) and YOUR SEAT (you: matches attended as stubs, your scorecards, and later your row — the people you sit with). Live match on? The site opens straight onto the ground. Nothing on? It opens on the next gate and your case.
